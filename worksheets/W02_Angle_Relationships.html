Worksheet 2: Angle Relationships

Session 2 — Complementary, supplementary, and vertical angles

Part 1: Complementary Angles (sum = 90°)

1 One angle is 35°. What is its complement?
2 One angle is 52°. What is its complement?
3 One angle is 78°. What is its complement?
4 Can a 95° angle have a complement? Explain why or why not.

Part 2: Supplementary Angles (sum = 180°)

5 One angle is 110°. What is its supplement?
6 One angle is 45°. What is its supplement?
7 One angle is 163°. What is its supplement?
8 An angle is supplementary to itself. What is its measure?

Part 3: Vertical Angles

9 Two lines cross. One angle is 72°. What are the other three angles?
10 Two lines cross. One angle is 130°. What is the angle directly across from it?

Part 4: 🏠 Roof Pitch Problems

A roof meets the wall of a house. The angle between the roof and the horizontal is called the pitch angle.
11 A roof has a pitch angle of 35°. What angle does the roof make with the vertical wall?
Hint: the roof, wall, and horizontal form a right angle at the corner.
12 A ramp goes from the ground to a porch. The ramp makes a 15° angle with the ground. What angle does the ramp make with the vertical edge of the porch?
13 Two sides of a roof meet at the peak. If each side makes a 40° angle with the horizontal, what is the angle at the peak?
A door is open. The wall and the fully-open door form a straight angle (180°).
14 If a door is open 65° from the wall, what is the remaining angle on the other side?
15 Two hallways meet at an intersection. One angle is 118°. Find all four angles at the intersection.
16 A ladder leans against a wall at a 68° angle from the ground. What angle does it make with the wall?
17 The hands of a clock at 4:00 form a 120° angle. What is the supplement of this angle?
18 Two angles are supplementary. One is 3 times the other. Find both angles.
19 Two angles are complementary. One is 20° more than the other. Find both angles.
20 A window has 4 angles. One angle is 90°. The opposite angle is also 90°. The remaining two angles are supplementary. If one is 75°, what is the other?

🔑 Answer Key

1. 55° | 2. 38° | 3. 12° | 4. No — complement would be negative, and angles can't be negative

5. 70° | 6. 135° | 7. 17° | 8. 90° (90 + 90 = 180)

9. 72°, 108°, 72°, 108° | 10. 130°

11. 90° − 35° = 55° | 12. 90° − 15° = 75° | 13. 180° − 40° − 40° = 100°

14. 180° − 65° = 115° | 15. 118°, 62°, 118°, 62° | 16. 90° − 68° = 22°

17. 60° | 18. 45° and 135° | 19. 35° and 55° | 20. 105°
