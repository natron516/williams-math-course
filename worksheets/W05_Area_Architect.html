Worksheet 5: Area Architect

Session 5 — Area of rectangles and triangles

Part 1: Basic Area

1 Rectangle: length = 14 ft, width = 9 ft. A = ____
2 Square: side = 7.5 m. A = ____
3 Triangle: base = 10 cm, height = 6 cm. A = ____
4 Triangle: base = 15 in, height = 8 in. A = ____
5 A rectangle has an area of 96 sq ft and a width of 8 ft. Find the length.

Part 2: 🎨 Room Makeover

You're redecorating a bedroom that's 12 ft × 10 ft with 8 ft ceilings. It has one window (3 ft × 4 ft) and one door (3 ft × 7 ft). Paint costs $35/gallon and covers 350 sq ft.
6 What is the total wall area? (4 walls, remember the height!)
7 Subtract the window and door areas. What's the paintable area?
8 How many gallons of paint do you need? (Round up — you can't buy half a gallon.)
9 What's the total paint cost?
10 How much carpet is needed for the floor? What if carpet costs $4.50/sq ft?

Part 3: More Real-World Area

🏗️ An L-shaped room: the main section is 15 ft × 10 ft, and an attached section is 8 ft × 6 ft.
11 Break this into two rectangles and find the total area.
12 How many 1 ft × 1 ft floor tiles are needed?
A triangular park has a base of 200 ft and a height of 120 ft.
13 What is the area of the park?
14 If grass seed covers 1,000 sq ft per bag and costs $24/bag, how many bags and what's the cost?
15 A rectangular driveway is 20 ft × 10 ft. Concrete costs $6 per sq ft to pour. How much will the driveway cost?
16 A rectangular tablecloth is 60 in × 84 in. Express the area in square feet.
Hint: 1 sq ft = 144 sq in

🔑 Answer Key

1. 14 × 9 = 126 sq ft | 2. 7.5² = 56.25 sq m | 3. ½(10)(6) = 30 sq cm

4. ½(15)(8) = 60 sq in | 5. 96 ÷ 8 = 12 ft

6. 2(12×8) + 2(10×8) = 192 + 160 = 352 sq ft

7. 352 − (3×4) − (3×7) = 352 − 12 − 21 = 319 sq ft

8. 319 ÷ 350 = 0.91 → 1 gallon | 9. 1 × $35 = $35

10. 12 × 10 = 120 sq ft, Cost = 120 × $4.50 = $540

11. 15×10 + 8×6 = 150 + 48 = 198 sq ft | 12. 198 tiles

13. ½(200)(120) = 12,000 sq ft | 14. 12 bags, $288

15. 200 sq ft × $6 = $1,200 | 16. 5,040 sq in ÷ 144 = 35 sq ft
