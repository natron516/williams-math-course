Worksheet 7: Going in Circles

Session 7 — Circumference of circles

Part 1: Basic Circumference (use π ≈ 3.14)

1 Diameter = 10 cm. C = ____
2 Radius = 7 in. C = ____
3 Diameter = 25 ft. C = ____
4 Radius = 3.5 m. C = ____
5 C = 62.8 cm. Find the diameter.
6 C = 44 in. Find the radius.

Part 2: 🚲 Bike Wheel Math

A bicycle wheel's circumference tells you how far the bike moves in one full rotation.
7 A mountain bike has wheels with a 26-inch diameter. How far does the bike travel in one wheel rotation?
8 A kid's bike has wheels with a 20-inch diameter. How far per rotation?
9 How much farther does the mountain bike go per rotation compared to the kid's bike?
10 The mountain bike rider pedals 500 rotations. How far have they traveled? Convert to feet.
Hint: 12 inches = 1 foot
11 How many rotations of the 20" wheel does it take to travel 1 mile? (1 mile = 5,280 ft = 63,360 in)

Part 3: More Circumference Problems

🎡 A Ferris wheel has a diameter of 60 feet.
12 How far do you travel in one full revolution?
13 The ride does 5 revolutions. Total distance?
🌳 A tree trunk has a circumference of 94.2 inches.
14 What is the diameter of the tree trunk?
15 A circular garden has a radius of 8 feet. You want to put a stone border around the edge. How many feet of stone border do you need?
16 A clock face has a diameter of 12 inches. How far does the tip of the minute hand travel in one hour?

🔑 Answer Key

1. π(10) = 31.4 cm | 2. 2π(7) = 43.96 in | 3. π(25) = 78.5 ft

4. 2π(3.5) = 21.98 m | 5. 62.8 ÷ π = 20 cm | 6. 44 ÷ (2π) = 7 in

7. π(26) = 81.64 in | 8. π(20) = 62.8 in | 9. 81.64 − 62.8 = 18.84 in

10. 500 × 81.64 = 40,820 in ÷ 12 = 3,401.67 ft

11. 63,360 ÷ 62.8 ≈ 1,009 rotations

12. π(60) = 188.4 ft | 13. 5 × 188.4 = 942 ft

14. 94.2 ÷ π = 30 in | 15. 2π(8) = 50.24 ft | 16. π(12) = 37.68 in
