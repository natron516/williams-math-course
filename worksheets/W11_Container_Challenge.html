Worksheet 11: Container Challenge

Session 11 — Cylinder volume and surface area

Part 1: Cylinder Volume (use π ≈ 3.14)

1 Radius = 5 cm, height = 10 cm. V = ____
2 Diameter = 8 in, height = 12 in. V = ____
3 Radius = 3 ft, height = 7 ft. V = ____
4 A cylinder has V = 502.4 cu cm and height = 10 cm. Find the radius.

Part 2: 🥤 Drink Container Design

Compare different cup and can shapes!
5 A soda can: diameter 2.6", height 4.8". What's the volume?
6 A tall glass: diameter 3", height 6". Volume?
7 A short wide mug: diameter 4", height 3.5". Volume?
8 Rank the three containers from most to least volume.
9 A water bottle is cylindrical: diameter 3", height 9". It holds how many cubic inches? How many fluid ounces? (1 fl oz ≈ 1.8 cu in)

Part 3: Surface Area of Rectangular Prisms

Surface area = total area of all faces. Think: how much wrapping paper or cardboard?
10 A box: 10" × 6" × 4". SA = 2(10×6) + 2(10×4) + 2(6×4) = ____
11 A cube with 5 cm sides. SA = ____
12 You're wrapping a gift box that's 14" × 10" × 3". What is the minimum wrapping paper area needed?
13 A cereal box is 12" × 8" × 2.5". How much cardboard is used to make it?
14 You're painting the outside of a toy chest (no lid): 3 ft × 2 ft × 2 ft. How many square feet to paint? (5 sides only!)
