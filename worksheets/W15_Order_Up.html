Worksheet 15: Order Up!

Session 15 — Order of operations with variables and recipe scaling

Part 1: PEMDAS with Variables

1 Evaluate 2x + 3 when x = 5
2 Evaluate 4(n − 2) when n = 7
3 Evaluate 3x² when x = 4
4 Evaluate (2x)² when x = 3
5 Evaluate 5 + 2(x + 1) when x = 6
6 Evaluate x² − 2x + 1 when x = 5
7 What's different about #3 and #4? Explain why they give different answers.

Part 2: 🍳 Recipe Scaling

A cookie recipe for 1 batch (24 cookies):
• 2 cups flour (f)
• 1 cup sugar (s)
• ½ cup butter (b)
• 3 eggs (e)

Expression for n batches: 2n cups flour, 1n cups sugar, 0.5n cups butter, 3n eggs
8 How much of each ingredient for 2 batches?
9 How much of each ingredient for 3 batches?
10 You want to make 60 cookies. That's how many batches? How much flour?
11 You only have 5 cups of flour. How many full batches can you make? How many cookies is that?
12 A brownie recipe calls for (1.5f + 0.75s + 0.25b) per batch. Evaluate for 4 batches.

Part 3: Multi-Step Expressions

13 Evaluate: 3(2x + 4) − 2x when x = 3
14 Evaluate: (x + y)² when x = 3, y = 2
15 Evaluate: 2x² + 3x − 5 when x = 4
16 Evaluate: (a + b)(a − b) when a = 7, b = 3
17 Simplify first, then evaluate for x = 2: 4x + 3(x − 1) + 5
18 A taxi charges $3.50 base + $2.25 per mile. Write the expression and evaluate for a 7-mile ride.

🔑 Answer Key

1. 13 | 2. 20 | 3. 3(16) = 48 | 4. (6)² = 36

5. 5 + 14 = 19 | 6. 25 − 10 + 1 = 16

7. 3x² = 3 × (x²) but (2x)² = (2x)(2x) — the 2 gets squared too!

8. 4 flour, 2 sugar, 1 butter, 6 eggs

9. 6 flour, 3 sugar, 1.5 butter, 9 eggs

10. 60÷24 = 2.5 batches, 2.5(2) = 5 cups flour

11. 5÷2 = 2.5 → 2 full batches = 48 cookies

12. 6f + 3s + 1b (6 cups flour, 3 cups sugar, 1 cup butter)

13. 3(10) − 6 = 24 | 14. 5² = 25 | 15. 32 + 12 − 5 = 39

16. (10)(4) = 40 | 17. 7x + 2, 7(2) + 2 = 16

18. C = 3.50 + 2.25m, C = 3.50 + 15.75 = $19.25
