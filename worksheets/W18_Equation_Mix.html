Part 2: 🏪 Lemonade Stand

You're running a lemonade stand! Lemons cost $0.25 each, cups cost $0.10 each, sugar costs $3 per bag (makes 20 cups).
9 You sold x cups at $1.50 each and made $36. How many cups? Write and solve.
10 Your total costs were $15.50. You made $42 in sales. What's your profit? (profit = sales − costs)
11 You want to make $50 profit. If costs are $18, what do total sales need to be?
12 You sold 30 cups. Some were regular ($1.50) and some were large ($2.50). You made $55 total. How many of each? (Set up the equation — this is a challenge!)

Part 3: Write Equations from Words, Then Solve

13 A number plus 17 equals 52. Find the number.
14 Eight times a number is 96. Find the number.
15 A number divided by 6 equals 11. Find the number.
16 After spending $35, Maria has $78. How much did she start with?

🔑 Answer Key

1. x = 25 | 2. n = 9 | 3. y = 64 | 4. m = 63

5. x = 6 | 6. p = 2.50 | 7. k = 100 | 8. n = 50

9. 1.5x = 36, x = 24 cups | 10. 42 − 15.50 = $26.50 profit

11. s − 18 = 50, s = $68 | 12. r + l = 30 and 1.5r + 2.5l = 55 → l = 10, r = 20

13. n + 17 = 52, n = 35 | 14. 8n = 96, n = 12

15. n/6 = 11, n = 66 | 16. m − 35 = 78, m = $113
