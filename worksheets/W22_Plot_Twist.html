Part 3: 🗺️ Treasure Map

A treasure map uses a coordinate grid. Each unit = 10 feet. North = +y, East = +x.
10 The old oak tree is at (2, 5). The big rock is at (−3, 1). How far apart are they east-west? North-south?
11 You start at the origin (0,0). Walk 4 units east and 3 units north. What are your coordinates?
12 From (4, 3), walk 6 units west and 5 units south. Where are you now?
13 The treasure is buried at the point that's exactly between (2, 6) and (8, 2). What are the coordinates?
Hint: average the x's and average the y's
14 Three clues are at (1,1), (5,1), and (3,5). If you connect them, what shape do they make?

Part 4: Mystery Picture

Plot these points in order and connect them to reveal a picture!
15 Star outline: (0,3), (1,1), (3,1), (1.5,−0.5), (2,−3), (0,−1), (−2,−3), (−1.5,−0.5), (−3,1), (−1,1), (0,3). What did you draw?
16 House: Start at (−2,0), go to (2,0), (2,3), (0,5), (−2,3), (−2,0). Then door: (−0.5,0), (−0.5,1.5), (0.5,1.5), (0.5,0).
17 What are the coordinates of a point that's 3 units directly above (−2, 1)?
18 What are the coordinates of a point that's 4 units to the left of (5, −2)?
19 If you reflect (3, 4) across the x-axis, what are the new coordinates?
20 If you reflect (3, 4) across the y-axis, what are the new coordinates?
