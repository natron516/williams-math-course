🐉 Multi-Step Problems
Problem 18 — Edmund's Turkish Delight
The White Witch smiled coldly and promised Edmund that if he brought his brother and sisters to her, she would make him a prince — and fill 3 whole rooms of her castle with Turkish Delight. Edmund's mouth watered as he imagined it:

  • The first room would have 9 shelves, each holding 12 boxes of Turkish Delight.
  • The second room would have 6 shelves, each holding 15 boxes.
  • The third room would have 4 shelves, each holding 18 boxes.
  • Every single box contained 8 pieces of Turkish Delight.
Step 1: Find the number of boxes on all the shelves in each room.
Step 2: Add the three rooms together to find the total number of boxes.
Step 3: Multiply by 8 to find the total number of pieces of Turkish Delight the Witch promised Edmund.
Show your work
Problem 19 — The Dragon's Hoard
When Eustace Scrubb slipped away from the others on Dragon Island, he stumbled into a dark cave and discovered an enormous dragon's hoard. Before he fell asleep on the treasure pile (and woke up as a dragon himself!), he greedily counted every piece:

Treasure Item Number of Items Value Each (gold coins) Total Value
Gold arm rings 8 15 _______
Jeweled goblets 6 24 _______
Bags of gold coins 12 30 _______
Diamond necklaces 4 55 _______
Silver swords 9 20 _______
Grand Total Value of the Hoard: _______
Step 1: Multiply each row to find the total value for that item.
Step 2: Add all five totals together to find the grand value of Eustace's dragon hoard.
