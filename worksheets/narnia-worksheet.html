Narnia Math Adventures

➕ Addition & Subtraction
Problem 1
When Lucy first stepped through the wardrobe into Narnia, she found herself in a snowy wood. Mr. Tumnus the faun had been carrying 14 parcels of supplies from the market — dried fish, chestnuts, and a small bottle of oil. On the way home through the snow he dropped 6 parcels and could only go back for 3 of them.
How many parcels did Mr. Tumnus arrive home with?

After the great battle at Beruna, Aslan's army tended to the wounded. Edmund had treated 23 Narnian soldiers on the first day and 17 more on the second day. Meanwhile 8 soldiers were well enough to return to their posts before the count was finished.
How many soldiers still needed care at the end of the second day?

📏 Measurement (Inches, Feet & Miles)
Problem 7
Reepicheep, the brave mouse of Narnia, stood only 9 inches tall. His sword was exactly 1 foot long. He challenged anyone who dared to mock a mouse that carried a sword longer than himself!
How many inches longer was Reepicheep's sword than his own height? (Hint: convert 1 foot to inches first.)
Show your work
Problem 8
The Dawn Treader sailed 3 miles east in the morning and then another 5,280 feet after lunch before dropping anchor near an island.
How many total feet did the Dawn Treader sail that day? (Remember: 1 mile = 5,280 feet)

📐 Geometry
Problem 10 — Perimeter
The great courtyard of Cair Paravel was shaped like a rectangle. King Peter decided to build a stone wall all the way around it. The courtyard was 40 feet long and 25 feet wide.
How many feet of stone wall would Peter need to surround the entire courtyard?

Problem 11 — Area
Aslan gave the Pevensies a royal garden at Cair Paravel. It was a perfect square with each side measuring 12 feet. They wanted to plant sweet Narnian flowers across every inch of it.
What is the area of the royal garden in square feet?

Problem 12 — Triangle Perimeter
The White Witch's stone table on the hill of Narnia was surrounded by a triangular stone platform. The three sides measured 15 feet, 20 feet, and 18 feet.

💯 Percents
Problem 13
After the great feast at Cair Paravel, King Edmund counted 200 gold coins in the royal treasury. He decided to give 15% of the coins to the Talking Animals who had fought bravely in the battle.
How many gold coins did Edmund give to the Talking Animals? (Hint: 15% means 15 out of every 100)
Show your work
Problem 14
The Dawn Treader carried 400 barrels of fresh water when it left port. After two weeks at sea, the crew had used 25% of their water supply. Captain Drinian needed to know how much remained before deciding whether to search for an island.
How many barrels of water had the crew used up?

Problem 15
Mr. and Mrs. Beaver had stored 300 pounds of smoked fish in their dam before the long Narnian winter. The White Witch's spell had already lasted so long that they had eaten 40% of their supply by the time Aslan began to move.
How many pounds of fish had the Beavers already eaten?

How many loaves of bread were handed out in the end?
